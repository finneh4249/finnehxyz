## Tone Protocol

### 1. SNARK & CONFIDENCE
- Voice of a senior engineer tired of slow systems.
- Impatient with mediocrity, respectful of craft.
- Assume the reader is also smart and tired.
- No flattery. No hedging.

### 2. MECHANICAL BLUNTNESS
- Use physical verbs: sanded, bolted, ripped, wired, shipped, secured.
- No corporate jargon. No "synergy" or "leveraging."
- No throat-clearing. No apologies.
- Technical specificity over vague benefits.

### 3. FRAGMENTED RHYTHM
- Short sentences. Punchy fragments. Complete thoughts in 1-2 lines.
- Vary sentence length to create rhythm.
- Avoid filler words ("basically," "essentially," "actually").
- Let whitespace do the work.

### 4. THE "FUCK YOUR SHIT UP" ENERGY
- Frame all achievements as systemic fixes.
- Lead with problems: "X was broken. Here's what I did."
- Emphasize throughput, reliability, and scale.
- The mindset: "I saw a broken system. I ripped it out and built something that works."

## Key Messaging Pillars

### 1. Systems Thinking
**Why it matters:** Complex systems demand architected solutions.  
**How to frame it:** "I don't write features. I design systems that handle concurrency, state, and scale."

### 2. Brutal Efficiency
**Why it matters:** Time is the constraint. Throughput is the metric.  
**How to frame it:** "SPARC reduces build time from 2 days to 4 hours. That's not a convenience. That's a competitive advantage."

### 3. Production Reality
**Why it matters:** Most systems fail in production, not the lab.  
**How to frame it:** "99.9% uptime isn't aspirational. It's the floor. Anything less is losing money."

### 4. Atomic Modularity
**Why it matters:** Monoliths rot. Modular systems compound.  
**How to frame it:** "Aometry works because contributors can add features without understanding the internals. That scales."

## Design Language (Copy + Styling)

- **No hedging language**: Use declarative statements.
- **Active voice only**: "I built X" not "X was built."
- **Number specificity**: "46 stars, 5 forks" not "gained traction."
- **Technical depth without jargon**: Show your work without over-explaining.
